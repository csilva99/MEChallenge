Improving the supply chain is an area where artificial intelligence can bring significant benefits to construction companies. AI can be used in various ways and provide numerous benefits:

Demand forecasting: AI can analyze historical data from previous projects as well as external factors such as economic conditions and seasonality to forecast future demand for construction materials. With these forecasts, the company can schedule orders and minimize shortages or excess materials, improving the efficiency of the supply chain. This enables constant communication with suppliers who may be instrumental in supplying products at a later stage of the projects.

Logistics: Artificial intelligence can be used to optimize the composition of transport containers and the logistics of delivering construction materials. AI algorithms can consider factors such as the final destination of the products, size, weight, shape, and physical composition to determine the most efficient way to pack the containers that will be later placed on ships for shipment to Africa. Monitoring and tracking of suppliers: AI can be used to monitor supplier performance in real-time, analyzing data such as delivery punctuality, material quality, and compliance with contractual requirements. This allows for quickly identifying issues and taking corrective actions, ensuring a continuous and efficient flow of materials throughout the supply chain. It also enables automatic and constant communication with suppliers to remind them of certain orders that are overdue and approaching the expected delivery time.

Prioritize tasks for automation: Select the administrative tasks that will bring the greatest impact initially, starting with tasks that are low in complexity and high in volume to achieve quick benefits and demonstrate the value of automation to the team.

Data-driven decision-making: With the help of AI, you can make more informed and strategic decisions in supply chain management. AI can analyze large volumes of data, identify patterns, conduct predictive analytics, and recommend actions to optimize supply chain efficiency, such as adjustments in orders, contract negotiations with suppliers, or changes in transportation routes. An example of this application in Mota-Engil's Procurement department is the potential forecast of product needs in ongoing projects and their material requirements, as well as suggesting possible GLOBAL CONTRACTS to be applied, allowing for optimization of not only financial resources but also time in the procurement of products identified by the AI model.

Team collaboration: AI-based collaboration platforms can connect all elements of the Procurement & Logistics team, providing real-time contact and information sharing about ongoing purchases. This allows the Logistics team to proactively plan shipments in the near future or make possible changes to already placed orders. This promotes efficient communication, facilitates collaboration among team members, and streamlines the decision-making processes.


How to apply?

To implement supply chain optimization using artificial intelligence in your construction company, the following steps should be followed:

  • Identifying specific needs and challenges of the company: Evaluate the pain points of the current supply chain, identifying areas where delays, inefficiencies, or lack of visibility occur. Determine which aspects of the supply chain can benefit from the implementation of artificial intelligence.
  • Setting objectives and goals: Establish clear goals for supply chain optimization, such as cost reduction, improved efficiency, better supplier management, waste reduction, or enhanced material traceability.
  • Assess available artificial intelligence solutions: Research and evaluate specific artificial intelligence solutions for supply chain optimization. There are various options, such as demand forecasting systems, route optimization, supplier management, data analytics, among others. Choose solutions that align with the needs of your company.
  • Data collection and analysis: Ensure the existence of suitable systems to collect and store relevant supply chain data. This includes data from suppliers, purchase history, demand, logistics, and other relevant data. The quality and integrity of the data are crucial to ensure accurate and reliable results.
  • Implementation and integration of the AI solution: Collaborate with AI solution providers to implement the technology into your supply chain. Ensure that the solution is integrated with your existing systems, such as project management, procurement, or logistics systems.
  • Preparation and empowerment: Provide adequate training to the team involved in supply chain management to effectively use AI solutions. Ensure that they understand how the algorithms work, how to interpret the results, and how to use the obtained information to make informed decisions.
  • Monitoring and continuous adjustments: Regularly monitor the results and performance of the AI implementation in the supply chain. Make adjustments as necessary to further optimize the processes and ensure that the established goals are achieved.
